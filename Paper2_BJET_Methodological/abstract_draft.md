## Title

Checkpoint-Mediated Agency Analysis: A Methodology for Studying Artificial Agency Effects in Multi-Agent AI Educational Systems

## Authors

Hosung You, Pennsylvania State University
Mohan Yang, Texas A&M University

## Abstract (~250 words)

**Research aims.** As agentic AI systems enter educational contexts, researchers lack validated methodologies for measuring artificial agency and distinguishing it from conventional tool-based assistance. This paper proposes Checkpoint-Mediated Agency Analysis (CMAA), a methodology that leverages the structured human-AI interaction points inherent in checkpoint-governed multi-agent systems as natural measurement instruments for studying artificial agency effects on educational decision-making.

**Context.** Grounded in Floridi's (2025) three criteria for artificial agency (interactivity, bounded autonomy, adaptability), we develop CMAA through the case of Diverga, a 24-agent AI research methodology assistant built on the Model Context Protocol. Diverga's architecture features mandatory human checkpoints, Verbalized Sampling (VS) for diversity-generating recommendations with Typicality Scores, and an immutable Decision Audit Trail, creating naturally occurring data about human-AI interaction at every critical research design juncture.

**Data and methods.** We present three methodological contributions: (1) a framework mapping multi-agent checkpoint architectures to Floridi's agency criteria; (2) five operationalized indicators for distinguishing agentic participation from tool-based assistance (Proactive Intervention Rate, State Utilization Depth, Decision Space Expansion, Governance Constraint Activation, and Coordination Complexity Index); and (3) a proof-of-concept embedded mixed methods study with doctoral students comparing tool-based and agentic conditions within subjects.

**Outcomes and conclusions.** CMAA enables process-level modelling of artificial agency effects through typicality trajectory analysis and sequential decision mapping, addressing the field's need for methodologies that capture how, not merely whether, agentic AI shapes learning. The proposed indicators and framework are designed for transferability across educational AI systems internationally, offering the research community replicable instruments for the emerging study of artificial agency in education.
