## The Opportunity

AI-powered tools are being rapidly adopted by graduate students for research design, yet we lack design frameworks for building AI tools that scaffold — rather than replace — researchers' methodological reasoning. Two known problems underpin this gap:

- **Mode collapse**: Generic AI tools repeatedly recommend the same familiar methodologies, homogenizing research design choices.
- **Automation bias**: Users tend to accept AI recommendations without critical evaluation, particularly when tools present a single "best" answer.

I have been developing **Diverga**, an open-source, MCP-based research methodology assistant with 44 specialized agents that addresses these problems through two mechanisms:

1. **Verbalized Sampling (VS)**: A technique that generates multiple alternative recommendations with typicality scores (T-Scores), making the "modal-ness" of each suggestion transparent.
2. **Human Checkpoints**: Structured intervention points where the system pauses and requires the researcher to make — and justify — their own decisions before proceeding.

Your recent work on orchestrating AI agents via the Model Context Protocol (Yang et al., 2025, *Education Sciences*) and your study of ChatGPT interaction patterns among nontraditional students (Yang et al., 2025, *BJET*) directly intersect with this space. I see a compelling opportunity to combine our perspectives.

## Proposed Research

### Title Direction

**"Designing Authentic AI Scaffolding for Research Methodology Learning: An LXD-Informed Design Case of a Multi-Agent Checkpoint System"**

### Core Question

Can AI research tools be designed to scaffold researchers' methodological reasoning through authentic learning principles, and if so, how?

### Why Our Collaboration Is Uniquely Positioned

| Dimension | Your Contribution | My Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Theoretical lens | Authentic Learning framework; Transfer of Training | Verbalized Sampling; Mode Collapse Theory |
| Technical platform | MCP architecture expertise (dynamic learner state) | Diverga platform (44 MCP-based agents) |
| Methodology | LXD, Design Case, Mixed Methods | EDR, Activity Theory, Conjecture Mapping |
| Institutional access | Texas A&M doctoral students | Penn State doctoral students |

The shared MCP foundation is particularly notable — you have theorized how MCP enables dynamic learner state orchestration, while Diverga is a concrete implementation of MCP-orchestrated research scaffolding. Together, we can bridge theory and practice in a way neither could alone.

### Proposed Design (Two Phases)

**Phase 1: LXD-Informed Design Case**
- Systematically document how Herrington's 9 authentic learning elements are operationalized in Diverga's checkpoint architecture
- You would lead the authentic learning analysis and LXD framing, providing an independent instructional design perspective
- I would provide the design rationale documentation and MCP architecture analysis

**Phase 2: Mixed-Methods Evaluation**
- Modeled on your ChatGPT study methodology (BJET, 2025)
- Pre/post research design tasks (with and without Diverga), think-aloud protocols, semi-structured interviews, and checkpoint log analysis
- Multi-site data collection at both Penn State and Texas A&M (N=12-16)
- Joint qualitative coding with independent first-round coding for inter-rater reliability

### Expected Contributions

1. **Authentic AI Scaffolding Framework** — first application of authentic learning theory to multi-agent AI research tools
2. **Evidence-based design principles** for checkpoint placement and VS integration in AI tools
3. **Checkpoint-as-Scaffold model** — repositioning human checkpoints from approval gates to epistemic scaffolds
4. **Multi-site validation** across two institutional contexts
