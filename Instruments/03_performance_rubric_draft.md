# Research Design Quality Rubric (Draft v1)

**Source**: Developed for the present study. Structure inspired by Yang et al. (2025, Appendix A) four-level rubric format. Content dimensions developed from standard research design pedagogy in social sciences (Creswell & Creswell, 2018; Maxwell, 2013).

**Purpose**: To provide a four-dimension, four-level rubric for independent performance evaluation of research design artifacts produced in the Claude baseline condition (Phase 0), the agentic AI novel task condition (Phase 2), and the agentic AI re-design condition (Phase 3).

**Use**: Evaluation is conducted by the Principal Investigator, who did not develop the agentic AI system, as an independent blind evaluator. Artifacts are presented in randomized order with identifying labels removed. Inter-rater reliability is established during a pilot phase with one external educational research methodologist. Cohen's kappa is reported.

**Scoring**: Each dimension scored 1-4. Total score range: 4-16.

---

## Dimension 1: Research Question Clarity and Specificity

| Level | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 1 (Emerging) | Research question is vague, unfocused, or unresearchable as stated. Key variables or constructs are unnamed or ambiguous. No boundary conditions specified. |
| 2 (Developing) | Research question identifies a topic area but lacks specificity about population, context, or variables. Partial focus; some elements left implicit. |
| 3 (Proficient) | Research question is clearly stated, names the key constructs, specifies a population or context, and is researchable with the proposed methods. |
| 4 (Advanced) | Research question is precise, focused, researchable, and aligned explicitly with the theoretical framework. Scope and boundary conditions are articulated. Sub-questions are specified where appropriate. |

## Dimension 2: Theoretical Grounding

| Level | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 1 (Emerging) | No theoretical framework named, or framework named without connection to the research question. |
| 2 (Developing) | Theoretical framework named and briefly described but not integrated with the research design. Used as label only. |
| 3 (Proficient) | Theoretical framework described and connected to the research question and methods. Framework shapes the choice of constructs and analytic approach. |
| 4 (Advanced) | Theoretical framework integrated throughout the design. Rationale for framework selection is explicit. Framework's affordances and limitations are acknowledged. Alternative frameworks considered and rejected with stated reasons. |

## Dimension 3: Method-Question Alignment

| Level | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 1 (Emerging) | Method choice misaligned with research question. Proposed method cannot plausibly answer the question as posed. |
| 2 (Developing) | Method choice partially aligned. Some elements of the method match the question, but gaps remain. Sampling or data sources underspecified. |
| 3 (Proficient) | Method choice appropriate for the research question. Sampling, data collection, and analysis are coherent with the question. Limitations are briefly noted. |
| 4 (Advanced) | Method choice optimal for the research question and justified with reference to methodological literature. Sampling, data collection, analysis, and limitations are fully articulated. Alternative methods considered. |

## Dimension 4: Ethical Consideration

| Level | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 1 (Emerging) | Ethical considerations absent from the design. No mention of consent, risk, confidentiality, or researcher positionality. |
| 2 (Developing) | Ethical considerations mentioned as a general category (e.g., "IRB will be obtained") without substantive engagement with study-specific risks or protections. |
| 3 (Proficient) | Ethical considerations discussed with reference to the specific population and methods. Consent, confidentiality, risk mitigation, and data handling are addressed. |
| 4 (Advanced) | Ethical considerations integrated throughout the design. Study-specific risks are identified and mitigated with concrete procedures. Researcher positionality and potential conflicts are acknowledged. Alignment with relevant ethical frameworks (Belmont, CARE, etc.) is articulated where relevant. |

---

## Total Score Interpretation

| Total Score | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 4-7 | Emerging research design; substantial development needed |
| 8-11 | Developing research design; multiple areas need strengthening |
| 12-14 | Proficient research design; ready for advisor review |
| 15-16 | Advanced research design; ready for formal proposal |

## Use in Cross-Case Analysis

Performance scores are reported per participant per condition in the Paper 3 case portraits. Score changes from Phase 0 (Claude baseline) to Phase 3 (agentic AI re-design of the same scenario) serve as a within-subject indicator of re-reasoning effects. Cross-case analysis examines whether participants with particular characteristics (e.g., higher methodology expertise, challenge-seeking disposition) show larger score changes than others.

## Notes for Pilot Testing

- Whether four dimensions is sufficient, or whether additional dimensions (e.g., feasibility, novelty, communication clarity) should be added.
- Whether the 1-4 scale provides sufficient discrimination among doctoral-level artifacts, or whether a 1-5 scale with a middle anchor would be preferable.
- Whether the rubric produces acceptable inter-rater reliability (Cohen's kappa >= 0.70 is the target) when applied to a pilot set of artifacts.
- Whether blind evaluation is achievable given that Claude artifacts and agentic AI artifacts may have stylistic differences that inadvertently reveal condition.
