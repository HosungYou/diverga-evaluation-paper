# AI Research Self-Efficacy Scale (Draft v1)

**Source**: Adapted from Avey, J. B., Luthans, F., & Jensen, S. M. (2009). Psychological capital: A positive resource for combating employee stress and turnover. *Human Resource Management, 48*(5), 677-693. As applied by Yang, M., Jiang, S., Li, B., Herman, K., Luo, T., Moots, S. C., & Lovett, N. (2025). Analysing nontraditional students' ChatGPT interaction, engagement, self-efficacy and performance. *British Journal of Educational Technology, 56*(6), 1973-2000.

**Purpose**: To measure participants' perceived self-efficacy in using a specific AI system for research design work immediately after a single session.

**Response format**: 5-point Likert scale (1 = Strongly Disagree, 2 = Disagree, 3 = Neutral, 4 = Agree, 5 = Strongly Agree)

**Administration**: Administered twice per participant, once immediately after Phase 0 (Claude baseline condition) and once immediately after Phase 2 (agentic AI condition). Participants substitute the name of the specific AI system for the placeholder \[SYSTEM\].

---

## Draft Items

Please rate your agreement with each statement about your experience using **\[SYSTEM\]** during this session.

1. I feel confident using \[SYSTEM\] to design research studies.
2. I can effectively express my research needs to \[SYSTEM\] and get useful ideas in return.
3. I am able to evaluate whether \[SYSTEM\]'s suggestions are appropriate for my research context.
4. I feel capable of integrating ideas from \[SYSTEM\] into my own methodological reasoning.
5. I am confident I can use \[SYSTEM\] productively for similar research design tasks in the future.

---

## Adaptation Rationale

Yang et al. (2025) used five items adapted from Avey et al. (2009) on a 5-point Likert scale. The original Avey et al. scale measures general self-efficacy in work contexts. Yang and colleagues adapted the item stems to reference ChatGPT-specific and course-specific experiences. The present study further adapts these items in three ways to accommodate the single-session research design context:

1. **System-specific wording**: The placeholder \[SYSTEM\] allows the same five items to be administered in both the Claude baseline condition and the agentic AI condition, enabling within-subject comparison of perceived self-efficacy across conditions.

2. **Session-level referent**: Yang et al.'s items referenced course-long experience; the present items reference "during this session" to fit the 30- to 60-minute session structure.

3. **Research-design specificity**: Where Yang et al.'s items referenced general coursework, the present items reference research design work specifically, matching the task participants complete.

## Notes for Pilot Testing

Three questions should be addressed during pilot testing:

- Whether five items are sufficient to detect within-subject differences between the Claude and agentic AI conditions, or whether a sixth item measuring confidence in overriding AI suggestions should be added to capture agentic-AI-specific self-efficacy dimensions.
- Whether item 3 ("evaluate whether \[SYSTEM\]'s suggestions are appropriate") is interpreted consistently across participants with different AI knowledge levels.
- Whether the 5-point Likert scale provides enough resolution for within-subject comparison or whether a 7-point scale would be preferable.

## Finalization

The scale will be finalized and locked in the Open Science Framework pre-registration before data collection begins. Any changes after pre-registration will be declared exploratory.
