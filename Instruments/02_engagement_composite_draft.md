# Engagement Composite Scale (Draft v1)

**Source**: Adapted from Skinner, E. A., Kindermann, T. A., & Furrer, C. J. (2009). A motivational perspective on engagement and disaffection. *Educational and Psychological Measurement, 69*(3), 493-525. With cognitive engagement items drawn from Fredricks, J. A., Blumenfeld, P. C., & Paris, A. H. (2004) and Halverson, L. R., & Graham, C. R. (2019). As applied by Yang et al. (2025).

**Purpose**: To measure participants' emotional, cognitive, and behavioral engagement during a single AI-assisted research design session, yielding a composite engagement score.

**Response format**: 5-point Likert scale (1 = Strongly Disagree, 2 = Disagree, 3 = Neutral, 4 = Agree, 5 = Strongly Agree)

**Administration**: Administered twice per participant, once immediately after Phase 0 (Claude baseline condition) and once immediately after Phase 2 (agentic AI condition). Composite engagement score is computed by summing item responses within each condition, following the scoring procedure used by Yang et al. (2025).

---

## Draft Items

Please rate your agreement with each statement about your experience during this session with **\[SYSTEM\]**.

### Emotional Engagement (2 items, Skinner et al., 2009)

1. During this session, I felt interested in what \[SYSTEM\] offered.
2. During this session, I enjoyed working with \[SYSTEM\] on the research design task.

### Cognitive Engagement (3 items, Fredricks et al., 2004; Halverson & Graham, 2019)

3. I thought deeply about the choices \[SYSTEM\] offered me during this session.
4. I tried to connect what \[SYSTEM\] suggested to what I already know about research methodology.
5. I made a serious mental effort to work through the research design task during this session.

### Behavioral Engagement (3 items, Skinner et al., 2009)

6. I actively participated in the research design task during this session.
7. I stayed focused on the task and avoided distractions during this session.
8. I put real effort into using \[SYSTEM\] during this session.

---

## Composite Score

Following Yang et al. (2025), the composite engagement score for each condition is computed by summing all 8 item responses (range: 8 to 40). Sub-scale scores may also be reported descriptively (emotional: 2 items, range 2-10; cognitive: 3 items, range 3-15; behavioral: 3 items, range 3-15).

## Adaptation Rationale

Yang et al. (2025) used items adapted from Skinner et al. (2009), Fredricks et al. (2004), and Halverson and Graham (2019) in a course-level context. The present study adapts these items in three ways:

1. **Session-level referent**: Items are rephrased from course-level ("throughout this course I felt...") to session-level ("during this session with \[SYSTEM\] I felt..."), matching the study's single-session structure.

2. **System-specific wording**: The placeholder \[SYSTEM\] enables the same items to be administered in both conditions, supporting within-subject comparison.

3. **Research-design context**: Where Yang et al.'s items referenced general coursework, the present items reference research design specifically, matching the task participants complete.

## Notes for Pilot Testing

- Whether 8 items is the appropriate length, or whether a shorter 6-item version would reduce participant burden while still producing a usable composite score.
- Whether the behavioral engagement items (6-8) produce floor effects (ceiling effects are also possible given participants are observed during the session).
- Whether the cognitive engagement items should include an item on connecting AI output to personal research interests, which is particularly relevant to doctoral researchers.
- Whether the emotional engagement items should include a negative-valenced item (e.g., "I felt frustrated during this session") to reduce acquiescence bias.

## Finalization

Item wording will be finalized and locked in the Open Science Framework pre-registration before data collection begins.
