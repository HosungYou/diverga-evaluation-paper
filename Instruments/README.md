# Instruments

Draft adaptations of measurement instruments for the multi-site research design specified in `Research_Design_IRB/`. All instruments adapt published validated scales or rubrics from Yang et al. (2025, British Journal of Educational Technology) for the single-session research design context of the present study.

## Purpose

All instruments serve the measurement plan specified in `Research_Design_IRB/Research_Design_IRB_v1.docx`, Sections 4.2 (Quantitative Strand) and 6.5 (Self-Report Measures). Instruments 01 through 03 are administered as self-report or scored by independent evaluators post-hoc. Instruments 04 through 06 are applied post-hoc by two independent coders to both the Claude.ai baseline and the agentic AI condition to support direct between-condition contrast at the Tier 1 level.

## Status and Review Process

These drafts are intended for Principal Investigator review prior to pilot testing. The planned pilot uses two external research methodologists to establish inter-rater reliability (Cohen's kappa) on the rubrics and to refine item wording on the self-report scales. Final instrument versions will be locked in the Open Science Framework pre-registration before data collection begins.

## Citation Requirement

Because all instruments except the performance rubric adapt published validated scales or rubrics, the original sources must be cited in every paper reporting results from these instruments. The present study cites Yang et al. (2025) as the immediate methodological predecessor and the original source publications for each scale.

## Coordination with Yang et al. (2025)

Because the Tier 1 rubrics and self-report scales were developed and first applied by Yang and colleagues in their 2025 BJET paper, and because Yang is a co-author on the present study, adaptations have been prepared for review by Yang before pilot testing. The goal is to ensure that the adaptations preserve the original operationalization to the extent possible while accommodating the single-session research design context of the present study.
